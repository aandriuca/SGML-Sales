You sell across more than one platform. Revenue is climbing. But *growing* and
*keeping more of what you grow* are not the same thing — and the gap between them is
where most multi-platform brands quietly bleed.

The leaks below rarely show up on your top line. They hide *between* platforms — in
the gaps where Shopify, Amazon and Etsy each settle, charge, and report differently,
and where no single dashboard sees the whole picture. Run this checklist on your own
business. For each one, ask the harder question: **can I answer this cleanly, by
channel, right now?** Every "no" is margin you're earning and not keeping.

---

## Part 1 — Your channels aren't telling you the truth

**1. Blended margin is hiding a losing channel.**
You look at overall margin and it's fine. But "overall" is an average — and averages
lie. On multi-platform brands, one channel is almost always carrying another.
→ *Spot it:* can you state the **net margin of each channel separately**, after that
channel's own fees and shipping? If you only know the blended number, you don't yet
know which channel is funding the business and which is draining it.

**2. Realized price isn't your list price.**
Coupons stack. Site-wide promos overlap with influencer codes. Marketplace deals
shave a few points you never feel — per order. The price you *set* and the price you
*actually realize* drift apart all month.
→ *Spot it:* pull your true **average realized price** per SKU after every discount,
and compare it to list. If the gap surprises you, your promos are pricing decisions
you never consciously made.

**3. Each platform's fees never make it into your margin math.**
Amazon referral + FBA fees, Etsy transaction + offsite-ads fees, eBay final-value
fees, Shopify app subscriptions — these sit *outside* cost of goods, so they silently
vanish from the margin you think you're earning.
→ *Spot it:* add up **every platform fee as a % of that channel's sales**. If it's not
a line you watch monthly, your "margin" is overstated by exactly that much.

## Part 2 — The platforms take more than their headline cut

**4. Shipping you don't fully recover.**
What you charge customers for shipping and what carriers actually bill you (with
dimensional weight, fuel and residential surcharges, peak-season rates) are two
different numbers. "Free shipping" is rarely free to you.
→ *Spot it:* compare **shipping income vs. shipping spend** for one month. If you're
under water, every order is a little smaller than it looks.

**5. Marketplace reserves and settlement timing.**
Amazon holds funds in reserve and disburses on its own clock — not in the period you
made the sale. It's easy to read a disbursement as "profit" when it's just cash
catching up.
→ *Spot it:* does your **earned revenue** match your settlements period over period —
or are you steering by whatever landed in the bank this week?

**6. Payment, chargeback and FX fees.**
Processor fees, chargeback penalties, and currency-conversion spreads on cross-border
sales each take a sliver. Individually tiny; in aggregate, real points of margin.
→ *Spot it:* total **fees + chargebacks + FX** as a share of revenue. Most owners
underestimate this by half.

## Part 3 — Inventory is leaking quietly

**7. Your COGS isn't fully landed.**
If freight-in, duties, customs brokerage and 3PL receiving aren't loaded into unit
cost, your COGS is understated — which means your margin is *over*stated, and you're
making decisions on a number that's wrong in your favour.
→ *Spot it:* does your cost per unit include **everything to get it on the shelf** —
not just the supplier invoice?

**8. Storage and aged-inventory surcharges.**
FBA long-term storage fees, aged-inventory surcharges, and 3PL storage quietly punish
slow movers. The SKU you're proud of holding might be costing you to keep.
→ *Spot it:* which SKUs have sat longest, and what is **storage costing you per unit
per month** on them? Dead inventory isn't neutral — it's a recurring charge.

**9. Returns and refunds cost more than the refund.**
A refunded order still cost you the original pick, pack and ship — plus return
shipping, plus inspection, restocking or disposal. The refund is only the visible part.
→ *Spot it:* track **refund rate by channel** and the *fully loaded* cost of a return.
If one channel returns at twice the rate, its real margin is far below its headline.

## Part 4 — The obligations that quietly become liabilities

**10. GST/HST and US sales tax handled by guesswork.**
Multi-province GST/HST and US sales-tax nexus are easy to get subtly wrong — either
over-remitting (handing back money you didn't owe) or quietly building a liability
that lands all at once.
→ *Spot it:* can you say **where you have an obligation, and that you're collecting and
remitting correctly** in each one? "I think so" is the answer that becomes a bill.

**11. Cash disbursements booked as if they were profit.**
When the books follow the bank instead of the sale, deposits look like earnings and
reserves look like losses. You end up managing cash timing, not profit.
→ *Spot it:* do your numbers tell you **what the business actually earned this month**,
independent of when each platform decided to pay you?

**12. The repeat-customer math — the skincare & supplements trap.**
For consumable brands, a subscriber or repeat buyer isn't one sale; they're a stream.
Treat first-order economics as the whole story and you'll either over-spend to acquire
or kill a cohort that was about to become your best one.
→ *Spot it:* do you know your **cost to acquire vs. the value a customer actually
returns over time** — by channel? Without it, your growth spend is a guess.
