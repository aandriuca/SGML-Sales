## The one rule (from `brand.py` → MESSAGING)

**We do not sell bookkeeping, tax, accounting, or "fractional CFO." We sell the
owner's transformation.** Centre every message on the founder and their world —
never on us or what we do.

In outreach terms:

- **Never name the service or list deliverables.** Don't open with "we handle books
  and CFO work" or "we do GST/HST and reconciliation." Imply our expertise *through*
  the clarity and edge we hand them.
- **Lead with who they become** once they can finally *see* their true numbers: the
  business grows stronger (not just busier), a calmer life with real control, and the
  standing they actually crave — visibly winning, respected among their peers.
- **Earn it with ONE concrete, grounded detail only we can give** — their true margin
  by channel, the hidden leaks (fees, shipping, refunds, reserves, COGS), what the
  business actually *keeps*. This is what keeps the promise credible instead of
  "we change lives" fluff.
- **Positioning is who we ARE, not the pitch.** That we work *only* with Canadian
  e-commerce operators your size is credibility we let show through the specificity of
  what we see — not a feature list we recite.

## How we sound (from `brand.py` → VOICE)

A sharp, credible advisor **with personality and range** — a smart, slightly witty
CFO friend, not a stiff corporate robot and not a cheesy meme account.

- **Vary the tone across the sequence** so it never reads like a template blast: one
  touch dry and witty, one warm and reassuring, one a quick story, one seriously
  diagnostic. (See the sequence in the playbook — each touch has a deliberately
  different register.)
- **Pull in broad e-commerce intelligence** so it isn't dry, then **always loop back
  to what it means for the owner** — their growth, their edge, their standing — never
  to a checklist of our services.
- **The hard line:** never cheap clickbait, fake urgency, or gimmicks that undercut a
  financial firm's credibility. Smart wit YES; "STOP scrolling!!", fake "last chance,"
  or a manipulative "should I close your file?" break-up — NO. We stay advisory and
  honest: never hype, never a guarantee.

## Outreach message shape (the formula, transformation-led)

Replaces the old service-led "observation → problem → proof → CTA" formula.

1. **See them** — a specific, credible observation about life as a multi-platform
   Canadian operator (Shopify + Amazon + the rest), not a problem lecture.
2. **The thing only clarity reveals** — one concrete, grounded detail: what each
   channel actually keeps, the leak hiding in plain sight, the number they can't
   currently see.
3. **Who they become** — imply the transformation that clarity buys: better decisions,
   a stronger business, the edge over peers. Let expertise show *through* it; don't
   name the service.
4. **Soft, low-friction CTA** — the free **Margin Leak Review** (the booked call =
   our conversion event), or the **Profit-Leak Checklist** for those not ready to talk.
   No fake urgency, ever.

## The two assets (shared with marketing)

Outreach and the marketing channel offer the **same two assets** so the funnel is one
system (see the Marketing Director Strategy, §4):

- **Top of funnel — "The Profit-Leak Checklist for Multi-Platform Sellers."** The
  lighter-touch capture for prospects not ready to talk; flows opt-ins into the
  newsletter + LinkedIn nurture.
- **Bottom of funnel — the free "Margin Leak Review" (30 min).** A mini-version of the
  service where we point at 1–2 real leaks in their numbers. This is the **primary
  CTA** on outreach and content alike, and the tracked conversion.

## Quick checklist before any message goes out

- [ ] Centred on the **owner and their world**, not on us?
- [ ] Did I avoid **naming the service / listing deliverables**?
- [ ] Is there **one concrete, grounded detail** (margin, leak, what they keep)?
- [ ] Does it imply the **transformation** they crave, credibly (no fluff, no hype)?
- [ ] **No clickbait or fake urgency**? Tone varied from the previous touch?
- [ ] CTA is the **Margin Leak Review** (or Checklist) — low friction?
